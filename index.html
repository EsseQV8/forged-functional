⚔ The Forge
The vow, and the two forges — what God ordains, and what I step into.
The Vow
I can bear what God ordains. I cannot bear what I cause. I will not be the author of my own inability to serve the people He gave me. The preparation is mine. The outcome is His.
⚙ The Forge I Don't Control
Providence
God uses every trial, delay, and hard providence as a forging instrument. What I cannot control, I trust is shaping me for good. I receive the hammer blows I did not choose.
🔥 The Forge I Step Into
Learn · Try · Fail · Attack
My edge is hunger to learn and refusal to quit. Try, fail, try, attack, fail, attack again. The pursuit of knowing deeper and pushing harder is fuel, not pressure.
The Law Beneath It
The fruit downstream is only as strong as the source upstream. I will not spend what I have stopped replacing. I will not confuse activity with accomplishment, or trust I will rise to the occasion — I keep the weapon ready so I am not found wanting when the moment comes.

Why this is the handle

A hammerhead without a helve is useless. The strength was never the point — it exists to shelter and serve the people gripping the other end. I keep myself able for them: the court with my son, the hike with a grandchild, the friend who needs me strong. When the helve loosens, the most powerful head swings wild.
